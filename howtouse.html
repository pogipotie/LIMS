Livestock Inventory Management System (LIMS)

A complete, COA-compliant guide to managing university livestock, custodians, transactions, and staff accounts.

1. Understanding System Roles

LIMS utilizes Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) to ensure data security and accountability.

  • Admin (Property Office): Has full access to the system. Can create user accounts, assign roles, approve or reject transactions, and execute permanent deletions (derecognition).
  • Staff (Data Encoder): Can view inventory, record daily logbooks, and submit "Pending" transactions (like births, deaths, or sales) for the Admin to approve. Cannot delete animals directly.
  • Custodian (Farm Worker): Has restricted access. Can only see the animals specifically assigned to them. Can record daily health logbooks for their animals, but cannot access transactions, reports, or global inventory.

2. Initial System Setup (Admin Only)

Before adding livestock, the Admin must set up the foundational data.

A. Manage Categories

Navigate to Manage Categories. Add the types of livestock you will manage (e.g., Cattle, Swine, Poultry, Goats). You cannot add an animal without a category.

B. System Users (Staff & Custodian Accounts)

Navigate to System Users to grant access to your team.

  • Create Account: Select the Account Role (Staff vs Custodian), enter the member's Full Name, Email, and a temporary Password.
  • Email Verification: The system sends a verification email to the staff. You can track their status in the "Registered Accounts" table (Verified or Pending).
  • Resend Confirmation: If the staff didn't receive the email, click the 3-dot menu and select "Resend Confirmation".
  • Password Reset: If a staff member forgets their password, select "Reset Password". An 8-digit OTP will be emailed to them. Enter that OTP into the system along with their new password to unlock their account.

Scenario: Hiring a New Farm Manager

Situation: The university hires "Maria", a new manager for the swine facility.

Action: The Admin goes to System Users and creates an account for Maria. The table shows Maria's status as Pending. Maria checks her email, clicks the verification link, and the Admin dashboard instantly updates her status to Verified. She can now log in.

Two months later, Maria forgets her password. The Admin clicks "Reset Password", asks Maria for the 8-digit OTP sent to her phone/email, types it in, and gives her a new password.

3. Managing Livestock Inventory

The Livestock Inventory page tracks every animal currently active on the property.

Adding an Animal

  • Click Add New Livestock.
  • Enter the official Tag Number, Name (optional), Category, Gender, and assign a Custodian.
  • Once saved, the animal appears in your active inventory.

The "Show Derecognized" Toggle

By default, the table only shows Active (living/present) animals to keep your workspace clean. If an animal is sold, dies, or is transferred, it disappears from this view.

  • To view historical data, click the Show Derecognized switch at the top right.
  • This reveals inactive animals, tagging them with DECEASED, SOLD, or TRANSFERRED.

Scenario: Managing a Herd of 20 Goats

Situation: The university acquires a mixed herd of 20 goats (10 male, 10 female) of varying ages.

Action: The staff member navigates to Add New Livestock and creates 20 individual records (e.g., `GT-001` to `GT-020`), specifying the exact age and gender for each goat. They assign them all to "Juan", the Goat Farm Custodian.

Result: Juan can now log in, go to the Inventory, and type "Female" into the search bar. The system instantly filters the 20 goats down to just the 10 females so he can plan his breeding program without getting confused by the males or the swine.

4. Transactions & COA Compliance

The Transactions module is the most critical part of LIMS. It strictly follows the Commission on Audit (COA) workflow for asset acquisition and derecognition.

A. Recording a Transaction (Acquisition)

  • Navigate to Transactions > Record Transaction.
  • Select the Livestock, set the Type to Purchase, Birth, or Transfer In.
  • (Optional) Upload the Inventory Custodian Slip (ICS) as a Supporting Document.
  • Click Save. It will appear as PENDING until an Admin approves it.

B. Recording Mortality or Loss (Derecognition)

Strict COA Rule (3-Day Limit): If an animal dies, is sold, or transferred out, it must be reported within 3 calendar days of the event. If you enter a date older than 3 days, the system will flag the submission as a "Late Report."
  • Select the Livestock, set the Type to Death, Sale, or Transfer Out.
  • Mandatory Document: The system will physically block you from saving unless you upload a Supporting Document (e.g., Veterinary Death Certificate, Notice of Loss, Official Receipt).
  • Click Save.

C. Property Office Approval (Auto-Derecognition)

Staff can only submit transactions. Only an Admin can approve them.

  • Admin reviews the transaction and the attached PDF/Image.
  • If valid, Admin clicks Approve.
  • Automation: The moment a Death, Sale, or Transfer Out is approved, the system automatically removes that animal from the Active Livestock Inventory (changing its status to Deceased/Sold).

D. Printing the Monthly Report

For physical signatures and audits, go to the Transaction Logs and click Print Report. The system will automatically hide all website menus and generate a clean, black-and-white A4 PDF table.

Scenario: Reporting a Dead Cow (COA Audit)

Situation: Cow COW-005 dies from an illness. A state auditor will review the inventory next month.

Action: Within 2 days of the death, the staff goes to Record Transaction, selects COW-005, and chooses "Death". The system warns them that a document is strictly required. They take a photo of the Veterinary Death Certificate and upload it.

Result: The Admin reviews the document and clicks Approve. The database trigger instantly removes COW-005 from the Active Livestock Inventory (auto-derecognition). Next month, when the auditor asks where the cow is, the Admin toggles "Show Derecognized", finds COW-005 with the red DECEASED badge, clicks on its history, and instantly downloads the attached Veterinary Certificate as proof.

5. Regular Monitoring (Health Logbooks)

The Health Logbook module is used by Custodians to log the daily/weekly condition of the animals.

  • Select an animal from the list.
  • Log its current Weight (kg), feed consumption, vaccinations, or general observations.
  • This maintains a historical timeline of the animal's growth and health leading up to a potential sale or harvest.

Scenario: Weekly Weight Tracking

Situation: The farm needs to ensure the swine are gaining weight properly before sale.

Action: The staff member weighs Pig `SW-012` on Monday and logs `45kg` in the Health Logbook. The following Monday, they weigh it again and log `48kg`.

Result: The system builds a chronological history. If the pig gets sick and loses weight, the logbook provides documented proof for the university vet to review before issuing any certificates.

6. Dashboard & Analytics

The Dashboard provides a high-level, real-time overview of the entire university's livestock operation. It automatically calculates totals without manual counting.

  • Total Animals: Displays the current active herd size.
  • Recent Transactions: Shows the latest pending or approved movements.
  • Quick Actions: Provides fast shortcuts to add new animals or record transactions.

Scenario: The Morning Briefing

Situation: The Head of the Agriculture Department needs a quick status update on the farm.

Action: The Admin logs in and looks at the Dashboard. They instantly see that the total active inventory is exactly 142 animals, and they notice there are 3 "Pending" transactions waiting for their approval.

Result: The Admin approves the 3 pending births, and the Dashboard instantly updates the active inventory count to 145.
